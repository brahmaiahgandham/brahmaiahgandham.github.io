About

Systems research, close to the metal

I'm a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Information Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Trento, Italy. I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering at Mahindra University, Hyderabad, under the supervision of Dr. Praveen Kumar Alapati, where my work centered on lock design and concurrent data structures.

My current research sits at the intersection of systems and memory architecture: designing and benchmarking synchronization primitives for multicore and NUMA machines, and exploring processing-in-memory (PIM) as a way to move computation closer to data. I contribute to ARCHYTAS, a European Defence Fund project building non-conventional hardware accelerators — optoelectronic, volatile and non-volatile PIM, and neuromorphic — to address the power, efficiency, and scalability limits of modern AI and cryptographic workloads.

Alongside this, I keep an active line of work applying machine learning to intelligent transportation systems, in particular decision-making pipelines for plug-in hybrid electric vehicles.

Focus areas

Research interests

Four threads that keep showing up in my work, from spinlocks to near-memory silicon.

Lock design

Adaptive and learning-based locks for multicore and edge hardware, tuned for throughput and energy.

Concurrent data structures

NUMA-aware, lock-free structures with epoch-based reclamation and versioned routing.

Processing-in-memory

Offloading cryptographic and compute-heavy kernels onto DPUs and near-memory accelerators.

ML for transportation

Decision-making and benchmarking pipelines for intelligent, electrified vehicle systems.

Projects

Ongoing and recent projects spanning memory hardware, concurrency, and transportation systems.

ARCHYTAS · PIM

CryptoPIM

DPU-based implementations of AES-128 and SHA-256 on UPMEM processing-in-memory hardware, with scalability benchmarking across tasklets, DPUs, and ranks — including one of the first published SHA-256 implementations on a real PIM system.

UPMEMPIMCryptography
Concurrent data structures

NAP-Map

A NUMA-aware partitioned lock-free map with per-NUMA skip-list shards, 128-bit stamped pointers, versioned routing, a cross-shard merge log, and two-level (DEBRA-N) epoch reclamation.

NUMALock-freeSkip lists
Edge computing

RISC-V adaptive locks

RLSpinLock and QuietQLock: reinforcement-learning-based and MCS-style locks designed and benchmarked on an 8-core RISC-V edge board (Banana Pi BPI-F3) for energy efficiency and throughput.

RISC-VQ-learningEnergy efficiency

QBD contention model

A finite-population, Markov-coupled Quasi-Birth-and-Death model of multi-threaded memory contention, with sparse transient analysis and adaptive backoff control — currently under journal revision.
